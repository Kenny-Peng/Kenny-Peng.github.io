One of the most problematic issues I have with DND 5E is with its combat. Simply, the turn order system is just too limiting as it is in its current state to allow for
the kind of role-playing and cooperation between players that I'd like to have. It's stilted and static with no room for any dynamic actions of comboing of actions that would could realistically happen in real combat. 

There are a variety of different kinds of turn order systems that have been homebrewed or made by Wizard's of the Coast either such as those in the Player's Handbook or other books. However, they all dance around the problem of being unable to properly roleplay in combat. Thus, I present my solution to this problem: Fluid Initiative.

<H2> My Solution - Dynamic Initiative</H2>

Combat remains in windows of 6 seconds per round

Roll initiative as normal/or possibly at the top of every round

Players or enemies can choose to delay their turn until a later position in the initiative queue but never earlier (sort of a modification to readying, but does not use their action)

- Those that delay can choose to take their turn in tandem with any friendly afterwards and synchronize actions/role play

Main Issue - ("end of your next turn" effects) 
- Solved by having them end on your regular initiative turn before delays

Solves the block intiaitve issue of giving massive advantage to the side that rolls first initiative 
Solves the lack of coordination and rp opportunities from normal initiative

I was also thinking about how to enable the option of retreating in combat
