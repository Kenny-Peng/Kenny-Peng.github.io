I was the sole person selected from a research course by college faculty to present at a research exhibition and awards ceremony for my proposal, "Virtual Reality and Augmented Reality and Their Effect in improving Rehabilitation Rates of Patients Suffering from Muscle Atrophy after Long-Term Bed Rest". This proposal I drafted led to me being a finalist for the Schwartzstein Prize for First-Year Research, and it's a great example of the type of innovative research that I often think of and my familiarity with virtual and augmented reality.
